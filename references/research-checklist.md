# Business Bible Research Checklist

## Website Pages to Browse Normally

- Homepage
- Product/platform overview
- Feature/module pages
- Solutions/use-case pages
- Industry pages
- Pricing/plans
- Customers/case studies
- Integrations/API
- Security/trust/compliance
- Mobile/app pages
- Help center docs if public
- Blog/resource hub
- Webinars/templates/tools
- Alternatives/comparison pages
- About/company/press
- Partners/channel pages
- Contact/demo/signup flow pages

## External Sources

- LinkedIn company page
- G2
- GetApp
- Capterra
- Trustpilot
- Reddit
- Product Hunt/Hacker News/forums when relevant
- Crunchbase/press/funding pages
- App stores when product has a mobile app
- Customer websites or partner directories
- Competitor comparison pages

## Facts to Capture

- Category and positioning
- Founding date, HQ, size, markets
- Funding and investors
- Leadership/founders
- Product modules
- Pricing model
- Target customer
- Buyer/end-user personas
- Use cases
- Customer names and metrics
- Integrations
- Security/compliance claims
- Support/service claims
- Competitors
- Review ratings and pain points

## Screenshot Rules

- Capture screenshots only where they add evidence or visual context.
- Always include the source URL beside the screenshot link.
- Prefer full-page screenshots for product, pricing, customer, security, and comparison pages.
- If a page is blank, gated, stale, or redirects unexpectedly, record that in the atlas.

## Analysis Prompts

Ask:

- What problem is the business really solving?
- What is the wedge use case?
- Who signs, who uses, who blocks?
- What pain is urgent enough to pay for?
- Where does the product create switching cost?
- What must be true operationally for this business to work?
- What would a new entrant copy, avoid, or differentiate?
- What would a salesperson need to know before a discovery call?
- What would a CEO need to know before building this?
